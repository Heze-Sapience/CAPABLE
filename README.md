# 🌍 CAPABLE Program

**Computational Access for Professionals in Applied Biosciences for Leveling Experiences**

---

## 🚀 Overview

**CAPABLE** is a computational initiative designed to provide **equitable access to molecular dynamics (MD) simulations** for researchers working in biosciences, particularly in under-resourced environments in Africa.

Many experimental labs generate valuable biological data but lack access to computational tools needed to interpret molecular mechanisms. CAPABLE bridges this gap by offering a **structured, scalable MD simulation pipeline**, enabling researchers to gain atomistic insight into their systems.

---

## 🎯 Mission

To democratize access to molecular dynamics simulations and empower researchers to:

* Understand biomolecular mechanisms at atomic resolution
* Strengthen experimental findings with computational insight
* Produce globally competitive, publication-quality research

---

## ⚙️ What CAPABLE Provides

* 🧬 Molecular dynamics (MD) simulations (peptides, ligands, proteins, RNA/DNA, membranes).
* 🧪 Complex MD simulations.
* 📊 Standardized MD analysis outputs including MMGBSA/MMPBSA where applicable.
* 📄 Publication writing guidance.

---

## 🧩 Project Tiers

CAPABLE uses a structured allocation model to balance accessibility and scientific impact:

### 🟢 Tier A — Pilot

* ~100 ns simulation
* Exploratory / feasibility studies

### 🟡 Tier B — Standard *(Recommended)*

* ~100-200 ns (multi-replica)
* Robust datasets for research

### 🔴 Tier C — Advanced

* 500–1000 ns (multi-replica)
* Advanced mechanistic insight

---

## 🧠 How It Works

1. **Submit Project**
   Researchers provide peptide/system details and scientific questions

2. **Evaluation & Scoring**
   Projects are assessed based on:

   * scientific clarity
   * feasibility
   * data availability
   * publication potential

3. **Tier Assignment**
   Projects are assigned to Tier A, B, or C

4. **Simulation Execution**
   Standardized MD workflows are applied

5. **Results Delivery**
   Complete datasets + analysis are shared
